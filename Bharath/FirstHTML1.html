Under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), punishments include death, life imprisonment, imprisonment (rigorous or simple), forfeiture of property, fine, and community service, with specific sections addressing various aspects of these punishments.

Here's a more detailed breakdown:

Types of Punishments:

Death: Death penalty is one of the punishments prescribed under BNS. Life Imprisonment: Imprisonment for the remainder of a person's natural life. Imprisonment: Rigorous: Imprisonment with hard labor. Simple: Imprisonment without hard labor. Forfeiture of Property: The court can order the forfeiture of property. Fine: Monetary penalty. Community Service: A new form of punishment introduced under BNS.

Relevant Sections:

Section 4: This section defines the various kinds of punishments. Section 7: Deals with rigorous or simple imprisonment, allowing the court to decide the type of imprisonment. Section 8: Addresses fines and liability in default of payment. Section 9: Limits punishment for compound offences to prevent double punishment for overlapping offenses. Section 10: Addresses doubtful judgments where guilt for multiple offenses is unclear. Section 11: Allows a court to add solitary confinement to a person's prison sentence if they commit a serious crime. Section 143: Provides provisions for strict punishment up to life imprisonment for the offence of human trafficking. Section 144: Provides for strict punishment for the offence of sexual exploitation of trafficked children.
